About Me

Hi, my name is Patricia Carrillo Barnes, known to most of my clients as “Pat.” I have worked since 1988 as a psychotherapist with adults and children struggling with a variety of issues.


I am licensed both as a Professional Counselor (LPC) and as a Marriage and Family Therapist (LMFT). I have also achieved Diplomat status with the American Psychotherapy Association.


I believe that life is much like that of butterflies, that change from caterpillar and finally to butterflies. The process causes a lot of pain for the butterfly, but how beautiful they are at the end of the process! If we can trust in working through the changes in our lives; then, like the butterfly, we can have a new beginning.

Together we can begin the journey toward a new beginning.

My Approach

When deciding on a therapist, it is especially important to feel a connection with the one you choose. My approach is caring, warm, and collaborative.

I work with individuals who experience anxiety or depression from relationships, family or work settings, or major life transitions. In addition, I help parents of children with special needs. Issues such as ADD, ADHD, and spectrum disorders can sometimes cause isolation, sadness and even anger. I create a safe and supportive environment in which you can talk about your situation without fear.

I will help you address those parts of yourself that get in the way. Through the healing process, you can learn to manage stress, cultivate mindfulness, and move from being stuck or overwhelmed to living a more fulfilling life. Trained in the IFS (Internal Family Systems) model of psychotherapy, I find this approach especially effective in helping people to understand themselves and the choices they make.

With years of experience and training in a variety of approaches, I can work together with you to explore and work through old patterns or unexpected crises. I have found that, by treating yourself with compassion rather than judgment, positive change happens.
